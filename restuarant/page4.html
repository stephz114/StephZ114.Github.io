About


Linda’s began in the 1980s when her parents, a Mexican immigrant family, opened a small tortillería in Manhattan. Known for handmade tortillas and authentic Mexican flavors, it became a local favorite. When Linda took over, the neighborhood had changed with rising rents and new faces. She adapted by transforming the shop into a modern Tex-Mex diner, renaming it "Linda’s" to appeal to both old and new customers. Despite the updates, Linda’s stays true to its roots. The tortillas are still made by hand, and her parents’ recipes remain central. For Linda, it’s about honoring tradition while embracing the new Manhattan.
